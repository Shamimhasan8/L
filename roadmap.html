Senior Engineer Mental Model · WS6 Step 6
🧠 How a Senior ML Engineer Thinks (Build This Into Your OS)
Why does this algorithm work?
Trace every tool to first principles. Gradient descent: why does it converge? When does it fail? What's the loss landscape assumption? Never use something you can't explain from scratch.
When does this system break?
Before building, ask: what's the failure mode? Docker → what if the model grows to 10GB? RAG → what if the query is ambiguous? Thinking in failure modes = production-readiness.
What assumptions are baked in?
Every model assumes something about the data distribution. Every MLOps pipeline assumes certain hardware. State the assumptions explicitly — this is how senior engineers communicate.
What's the end-to-end system?
Never think in components. Think: data pipeline → feature store → training → serving → monitoring → feedback loop. Where is the bottleneck? That's where to focus.
How does this create business value?
Your recommender gave +10% sales lift. Your Bengali diarizer achieved DER 0.286. Always attach a metric. Engineers who speak in business impact get hired first.
Could I explain this to a non-engineer?
If you can't explain RAG to a product manager in 2 minutes, you don't fully understand it. Technical writing forces this clarity — it's why it's Gap #4 in your skill plan.
