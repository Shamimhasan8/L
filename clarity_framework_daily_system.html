The clarity equation
Before ANY AI interaction, answer these 3 questions
If you can't answer them clearly, you're outsourcing responsibility — not using AI.
1. What problem am I actually solving?
Not "I want to improve my resume." Specifically: "The framing of my Bangla Diarizz project doesn't communicate the constraint navigation story — I need to fix that."
2. Why does solving this matter to my leverage?
Not "it'll help." Specifically: "This is the one project that proves production thinking. Fixing its framing directly impacts my Kaedim and Realfy applications this week."
3. What would success look like?
Not "better." Specifically: "Three bullets rewritten so that latency constraint, trade-off decision, and measurable impact are each visible in one sentence."

The tolerance filter
The real divide isn't intelligence — it's tolerance for discomfort
Escaping discomfort
Ask AI for the answer. Wait until "ready." Polish before shipping. Avoid being wrong.
Compressing discomfort
Fail faster. Learn faster. Iterate faster. Launch ugly and let reality teach you.
Your ugly launch practice:
Ship the Bengali speech demo when the endpoint works — not when it's perfect. Push to HuggingFace Spaces with basic Streamlit UI this week. Let real users find the bugs. Reality teaches faster than planning.
